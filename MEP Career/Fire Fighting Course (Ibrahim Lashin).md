the chain reaction is the new element in the fire tetrahedron
- when ignition point is higher than boiling point, a chain reaction happens and the fire keeps on going
and in these fires we use <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">dry powder</span> system 
- will be talked about in the upcoming lectures

water is usually used to break the fire (<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">by breaking the heat element</span>)because
- water is cheap
- has a high cooling rate (high specific heat capacity)
- can be pressurized
but cannot be used in some gases
- gas fires because gas has lower density than water
- hospitals with costly equipment 
- money banks or important paper
- electric rooms

if we reduce the oxygen lower than 16%, then the fire gets put off, <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">how ?</span>
other gases are used to break the oxygen part of the fire in the previous places
- CO2
- FM200
- Novec
- Aresol
- Argon
	- they must have higher density than air
	- they must not be able to burn
	- used wherever we can't use water
	- can't be used in open spaces because we can't reduce the oxygen ratio than 16%.
	- can't be used wherever people are, because people need at least 14% oxygen ratio to breathe

in Petrol fires, we can't use gases in open gas fires but we can use<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">  the foam system</span>
- we mix foam (a chemical compound) with water with a ratio of 3 to 6 % to have the a mixture with lower density than the petrol or the burning liquid gas
	- here we get two privileges, we are breaking the oxygen because of lower density of the mixture than water but more than air and the heat factor because water has high specific heat capacity

## Siamese Connection
![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250513214143395.webp]]
- siamese connections is used by civil defense to supply water to the system
- it is two pipes as shown in each  siamese connection, where each inlet is 2 and half inch going both into a 4 inch pipe
- if my pump is 1000 gpm then 1 will supply 4 pipes, each pipe I will supply by 250 gpm reaching the 1000 gpm mark
- the main pipe for siamese connection (in this case the 4 inch) must be the same size as the riser
- ![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250513214738918.webp]]![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250513214843437.webp]]
- siamese connections has more names: <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">cut off or breeching inlets</span>
- so basically each riser has its own siamese connections
- if the riser is 4 inch then we need two siamese inlets as shown in photos
- if riser is small (3") for some small building then the code allows to use one siamese inlet only <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">(needs to be check with Dubai code)</span>

![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250518144637501.webp]]
- maximum height for siamese connection from ground is 1.2 m
- minimum height for siamese connection from ground is 0.5 m

- system component layout for siamese connections
- it is prohibited in NFPA (American code) and Egyptian code to put any isolating valve (while it is allowed in the European code) on the siamese connection, instead a check valve is used after the siamese connection
- pressure for Civil defense cars should be 12 bar , while in Egypt it is usually 7 bars.
- if the pressure is not enough to supply for the system in high rise buildings, the siamese connection is used to supply water for the tanks in the building and then building pumps are used to supply water to the fire location

## 7.1 Wet Pipe System

- this system is used for places where temperature is more than 4 deg C and less than 70 deg C

- the alarm check valve is used to control the wet pipe system
- components:
	- pressure switch
	- Gong bell
	- retard chamber
	- water motor to operate the water gong
	- pressure gauges
	- drain valves
	- bypass with swing
- before the ACV exists an OS&Y Gate valve
- the ACV also acts as a <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">check valve that allows only the flow in one direction</span> 
- main function is allowing the water to flow during fire and gives a mechanical and electrical signal alarm notifying the system of the fire 


### main working idea after a sprinkler is broken
- a sprinkler is broken.
- the pressure decreases in the lines.
- the jockey pump feels this loss of pressure and starts to work.
- after some time, if the jockey pump can't keep up with the needed flowrate to stabilize the pressure in the system.
- the electric pumps starts.

![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521180814506.webp]]
- the retard chamber is used as a way to delay the starting of the electric pump as much as possible
- the retard chamber has two lines to check the pressure in the system and one outlet to the pressure switch and the gong bell
- the retard chamber check the pressure before and after the check valve
	- if the jockey pump can stabilize the difference in pressure, then this means that this is a small leakage not a broken sprinkler
	- if the jockey pump can not do this, then over a small time the retard chamber is filled and gives a signal to the pressure switch and the alarm gong 
	- the fire alarm is on
	- the pressure <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">switch</span> is responsible to turn on the electric pump
	- in a case where the retard chamber is absent, the electric pump may work over a small leakage , then it switches off when the pressure is met, then work again when the pressure reduces and so on, this is costly and may induce permanent damage on the pump

Remember that the ZCV has a flow switch responsible for informing which floor has the broken sprinkler

## 7.2 Dry Pipe System
![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521192108030.webp]]
- this system is the same as wet pipe system except the part before the dry pipe valve <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">DPV</span> 
- before the DPV, the system is pressurized with water
- after the DPV, the system is pressurized with air or nitrogen
- user for system where temperature is less than 4 deg C so water doesn't freeze
- <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">notice that the siamese connection is put before the Dry pipe valve</span> from PERPLEXITY AI: 
	- For both wet and dry pipe sprinkler systems, the Siamese (Fire Department) connection is installed on the system riser, but its exact location relative to key valves differs:
	- **Wet Pipe System:** The Siamese connection is connected on the system side (downstream) of the main gate valve in the riser. This means it is after the main control valve but before the alarm check valve (ACV), allowing water supplied by the fire department to enter the sprinkler system directly
	- **Dry Pipe System:** The Siamese connection is connected between the dry pipe valve (DPV) and the main gate valve. This places the connection on the system side of the dry pipe valve, so when the fire department supplies water, it bypasses the DPV and enters the system piping directly
	- This arrangement ensures that, in both systems, the fire department can supplement water supply efficiently, but in the dry system, the connection is specifically placed to allow water into the piping network after the dry pipe valve has opened or been overridden.
- when the sprinkler is broken all the pressurized gas is let out![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521195946251.webp]]
- then the water presses on the dry pipe valve gate, then the water is let out and goes to the broken sprinkler
- DPV is also a check valve
- there is also a water motor gong to give a signal that there is fire and <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">this is considered a mechanical alarm</span> 
-  <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">there is also electrical alarm using a pressure switch</span> 
- drain valve is also present in the DPV
- pressure switches are also installed before and after the valve along with their ball valves
- note that the DPV has two face, one to the water, another to the air
	- the size of DPV exposed to water is small, so water force pressing is small
	- size of DPV exposed to wait is large, so air force pressing on the valve is big
	- so we don't need to achieve the same air pressure as water, as this would need a very big compressor or a multi-stage compressor
- notice that after a sprinkler is broken, water takes some time to reach the sprinkler.
	- the code has a regulation of maximum 60 sec for the water to reach the fire sprinkler
	- that's why an accelerator is used (usually when the pumps are larger than 500 gpm), <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">from PERPLEXITY AI</span> 
		- <span style="color:rgb(0, 176, 80)">An accelerator is used in dry pipe sprinkler systems to **speed up the opening of the dry pipe valve** when a fire occurs. In a dry pipe system, the pipes are filled with pressurized air or nitrogen, and water is held back by the dry pipe valve. When a sprinkler head opens due to heat from a fire, the air pressure in the pipes drops. Without an accelerator, it can take a significant amount of time for the air pressure to fall enough to open the valve and allow water to flow into the piping, especially in larger systems, because air is compressible and escapes relatively slowly</span>
		- <span style="color:rgb(0, 176, 80)">The accelerator detects a rapid and steady drop in air pressure—such as when a sprinkler activates—and quickly transmits this pressure change to the intermediate chamber of the dry pipe valve. This action causes the valve to trip much faster, allowing water to enter the system piping sooner and reach the open sprinkler heads more quickly The use of an accelerator ensures that water is delivered within the time required by fire codes (such as NFPA 13), typically within 60 seconds, even in large or complex dry pipe systems</span> 
### system working steps in case of fire in dry pipe system
1. fire happens, sprinkler break, then air or nitrogen is let out and the pressure downstream of the DPV decreases![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521201559236.webp]]
2. the compressor tries to compensate the loss in pressure.![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521201649908.webp]]![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521201712274.webp]]
3. pressure still can't stabilize so the air is then let to the side of the valve to accelerate the opening of the DPV to let the water in the system.![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521201802718.webp]]
4. water is let in the valve and then goes on to fill the pipes ![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521201825158.webp]]
5. the water is then let out from the DPV to go 
	1. control panel which gives electrical alarm of the fire![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521202012708.webp]]
	2. water gong motor to give a mechanical alarm of the fire![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521202024837.webp]]
6. the jockey pump tries to compensate for the loss in water pressure, then gives the signal to turn on the electric pump ![[Fire Fighting Course (Ibrahim Lashin)-20250521202247791.webp]]
7. then the system is fully working to put out the fire at the fire location

# Lecture 8 Deluge Sprinkler System

## Pre-action sprinkler system
same as deluge 
- but the pipes after the deluge valve is pressurized with air, instead of being at atmospheric pressure
- the sprinklers used in pre-action are closed sprinklers , in opposite to the open sprinklers used in deluge system
same as dry pipe
- but the control valve used in dry system is called dry pipe valve, but in pre-action system is the same as the deluge system control valve (called deluge valve)

**Key Takeaway:**

- Dry pipe systems provide localized fire control and protection against pipe freezing.
- Deluge systems deliver rapid, blanket protection for high-hazard environments.
- Pre-action systems offer extra safeguards for assets sensitive to water damage thanks to their two-step activation, minimizing accidental discharge risk.

Notice that pre-action is expensive due to its increased precaution system
- the pre-action air is pressurized with small pressure value, in order not to block the water passing when the signal is given to release the water from the pre-action valve
- the pressure is used to detect when the sprinkler bulb is broken in case of fire to give a mechanical signal to the pressure switch which in turn will give a signal to the control panel
- the control panel will not release the water unless an electrical signal also comes from a detector

- single interlock: works when the sprinkler bulb is broken , even if it is broken by mistake
- non-interlock: the system works is sprinkler is broken or if electrical signal is given by detector
- double interlock: double signals are needed, by sprinkler and detector to release the water
