## 1.URL Encoding
Link for encoder and decoder : https://www.albionresearch.com/tools/urlencode

![[URL Encoding-20251111123348279.webp]] 
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">The encoding that transforms the `@` symbol into `%40` is known as **URL encoding** (also called percent-encoding).</span>

In URL encoding:

- Special characters are replaced by a percentage sign (`%`) followed by two hexadecimal digits that represent the character's ASCII value.
- <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">For example, the ASCII value for `@` is `64`, which is `40` in hexadecimal. Thus, `@` is represented as `%40`.</span>
- @ = %40
